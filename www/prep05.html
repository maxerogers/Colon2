FAQ

1. How long after starting the Prep should my child start to go to the bathroom?

Each child is different, but most children should have to move their bowels by the time they finish both halves of the Miralax preparation. If your child has finished the entire bowel prep and still has not had a bowel movement, please wait another 2 hours. If he or she still has not gone to the bathroom, please call (631) 444-6000 and ask to speak to the pediatric gastroenterologist fellow on call.

2. My child drank the prep and is going to the bathroom, but it is solid or soft and not “apple juice”-colored. What should I do?

As long as your child continues to have bowel movements there is no need for concern.

3. My child drank the prep and has still not gone to the bathroom. What should I do?

If your child has finished the entire bowel prep and still has not had a bowel movement, please wait another 2 hours. If he or she still has not gone to the bathroom after waiting, please call (631) 444-6000 and ask to speak to the pediatric gastroenterologist fellow on call.

4. How long must my child refrain from eating before the colonoscopy?

Your child must not have anything to eat for 8 hours prior to arrival at the endoscopy suite.

5. When will I be able to get results from my child’s colonoscopy?

Biopsy results are usually available two weeks after your child’s procedure.

6. My child will be in school the day before the colonoscopy. At what time should he/she begin the prep?

If your child is going to school on the day before the colonoscopy, we recommend starting the prep when they return home from school.

7. The instructions say to eat nothing on the morning of the colonoscopy, but my child takes daily medications. What should I do?

Please ask your prescribing physician if it would be ok to delay taking the medication until after the procedure is finished. If the medications cannot be delayed, you can give the medications with a small amount of plain water.
